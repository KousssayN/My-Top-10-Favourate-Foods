Makroudh

Ingrediants

Sweet semolina cookies filled with dates, soaked in honey.

  1. Dough
    • 500g fine semolina
    • 200g butter, melted
    • 100g plain flour
    • 2 tbsp baking powder
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • Salt
  2. Filling & Coating
    • 400g pitted dates, chopped
    • 2 tbsp butter
    • 1 tbsp orange blossom water
    • Whole cloves for decoration
    • 400ml honey for soaking
How to cook
  1. Prepare the dough
    • Mix semolina, flour, baking powder and salt in a bowl. Gradually add melted butter and vanilla, mixing until the dough holds together; rest briefly if needed.
  2. Make the date filling
    • Cook chopped dates with a little butter and orange blossom water over low heat until soft and spreadable; mash to a smooth paste.
  3. Shape the Makroudh
    • Roll a portion of dough into an oval, press a strip of date paste into the center and fold to enclose the filling.
    • Seal and press decorative ridges with a fork or special mold; top with a whole clove if desired.
  4. Bake and soak
    • Bake at 180Â°C for 12â€“15 minutes until golden. While still warm, dip or drizzle with honey and allow to cool so the honey soaks into the pastry.

Ingrediants

Crispy Tunisian pastry filled with egg, meat, and potatoes, fried until golden.

  1. Pastry & Oil

    • Vegetable oil for frying
  2. Filling
    • 2 eggs

    • 1 medium potato, finely diced
    • 1 small onion, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tbsp harissa
    • 1/2 tsp caraway seeds
    • Salt and pepper
    • Fresh parsley and mint
How to cook
  1. Prepare the potatoes
    • Boil diced potatoes until tender, drain and let cool slightly so they keep their shape.
  2. Cook the filling
    • Heat oil in a pan, sautÃ© onion and garlic until translucent. Add ground meat or crumbled merguez and brown.
    • Stir in harissa, caraway, salt and pepper; cook 4â€“6 minutes until flavors combine.
    • Fold in the cooked potatoes and chopped parsley and mint; adjust seasoning to taste.
  3. Assemble the brik
    • Place a malouf (phyllo) sheet flat, spoon the filling in the center and create a small well for the egg.
    • Crack an egg into the well, fold the sheet into a triangle or envelope and press the edges to seal firmly.
  4. Fry and serve
    • Heat oil to mediumâ€“high and deep-fry the brik until golden brown on both sides (about 2â€“3 minutes per side).
    • Drain on paper towels and serve immediately with lemon wedges.

Malawi

Ingrediants

Traditional Tunisian flaky flatbread made from fine semolina, rolled and folded into layers, perfect for wraps or served with honey.

  1. Dough
    • 500g fine semolina
    • 100g flour (optional)
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 5g instant yeast (optional)
    • 300ml warm water
    • Olive oil for kneading and coating
    • Optional: sesame seeds, fennel seeds, black sesame seeds
  2. For Cooking
    • Olive oil or melted butter for layering
    • Non-stick pan or griddle
  3. Optional Fillings
    • Tuna, harissa, cheese, eggs, or honey
How to cook
  1. Make the dough
    • In a large bowl, mix fine semolina, flour, salt and optional yeast and seeds. Gradually add warm water while kneading by hand or with a mixer until a smooth, pliable dough forms.
  2. Rest the dough
    • Divide dough into small golf-ball-sized portions. Lightly coat each ball with olive oil, cover and let rest for at least 20â€“30 minutes to relax the gluten.
  3. Flatten and layer
    • On a lightly oiled surface, take one ball and flatten it with your fingers into a very thin, almost translucent sheet. Brush generously with olive oil or a mix of oil and melted butter.
    • Fold the flattened dough like an envelope or letter (fold into thirds, then fold into a square). This creates the characteristic flaky layers.
  4. Cook the flatbread
    • Heat a dry, non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat. Gently flatten the folded dough square slightly and place in the hot pan. Cook for 1â€“2 minutes per side until golden brown with light charred spots. The bread is oily enough that you typically don't need to add more oil to the pan.
  5. Serve
    • Stack cooked mlewi and keep wrapped in a tea towel to keep them soft and pliable. Serve warm on their own, drizzled with honey, or stuffed with tuna, harissa, cheese, eggs or other fillings to make a savory sandwich.

Kamoniya

Ingrediants

Traditional Tunisian liver stew with tomatoes and aromatic spices.

  1. Main Ingredients
    • 800g lamb or beef liver (kebda), cubed
    • 3 onions, chopped
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 400g crushed tomatoes
    • 3 tbsp tomato paste
    • 2 tbsp harissa
    • 2 tsp caraway seeds
    • 1 tsp coriander seeds
    • 300ml beef broth
    • Olive oil
    • Salt and pepper
How to cook
  1. Prepare the liver
    • Cube the liver and pat dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.
  2. Brown the liver
    • Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat and brown the liver pieces on all sides; remove and set aside.
  3. Build the base
    • In the same pot, add chopped onions and cook until soft, then add minced garlic, tomato paste and harissa; cook for 3â€“4 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add tomatoes and broth
    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes and beef broth, then return the browned liver to the pot.
  5. Season and simmer
    • Toast caraway and coriander seeds briefly in a dry pan, then add to the pot along with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer gently for 40â€“50 minutes until liver is tender and the sauce is rich and flavorful.
  6. Finish and serve
    • Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve hot in bowls with crusty bread, or alongside couscous for soaking up the delicious sauce.

Fricassee

Ingrediants

Fried sandwich filled with tuna, capers, and harissa - a Tunisian street food classic.

  1. Bread & Filling
    • 400g can tuna in oil
    • 2 tbsp capers, chopped
    • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
    • 1 tbsp harissa
    • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
    • Fresh parsley
    • Thin bread rounds or flatbread
  2. For Frying
    • Vegetable oil
    • 1 egg, beaten (for sealing)
How to cook
  1. Prepare the filling
    • Drain the tuna and mix with chopped capers, diced hard-boiled eggs, harissa and mayonnaise. Add chopped parsley and season to taste.
  2. Assemble the sandwich
    • Place a portion of filling in the center of a thin round of bread or flatbread and fold or close it, sealing the edges with a little beaten egg.
  3. Fry until golden
    • Heat oil in a deep pan to medium-high and fry the filled breads until golden brown and crisp on both sides; drain on paper towels.
  4. Serve
    • Serve hot with lemon wedges and a simple salad or pickles on the side.

Couscous

Ingrediants

Traditional Tunisian couscous with slow-cooked meat, vegetables, and chickpeas.

  1. Couscous Base
    • 500g couscous semolina
    • Salt
    • Water
  2. Stew
    • 800g lamb or beef, cubed
    • 4 carrots, chunked
    • 4 turnips, chunked
    • 2 onions, quartered
    • 2 cups chickpeas
    • 3 tbsp tomato paste
    • 2 tbsp harissa
    • 2 tsp caraway seeds
    • Salt and pepper
    • Olive oil
How to cook
  1. Brown the meat
    • Heat olive oil in a large pot and brown the meat on all sides to develop color and flavor.
  2. Build the stew
    • Add onions, tomato paste and harissa; cook a few minutes until fragrant.
    • Add carrots, turnips, chickpeas and caraway seeds; stir to combine.
    • Cover with water or broth, bring to a simmer and cook gently for about 1â€“1.5 hours until meat is tender.
  3. Prepare the couscous
    • Place couscous in a large bowl, sprinkle with a little salt and pour hot broth gradually while fluffing with a fork until light and separate.
    • Steam briefly if using a couscoussier for extra fluffiness.
  4. Assemble and serve
    • Mound the couscous on a serving platter, arrange the meat and vegetables on top and pour some of the cooking broth around the base.
    • Serve family-style so diners can spoon broth over their portions as desired.

Slata Mechouia

Ingrediants

Roasted vegetable salad with charred peppers, tomatoes, and onions.

  1. Vegetables
    • 4 red peppers
    • 4 tomatoes
    • 2 onions
    • 3 cloves garlic
    • 2 dried chili peppers
  2. Dressing
    • 4 tbsp olive oil
    • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
    • 1 tsp caraway seeds
    • Fresh parsley and mint
    • Salt and pepper
How to cook
  1. Char and steam the peppers
    • Char peppers over an open flame or under the broiler until the skins blacken. Place them in a sealed bag for 10 minutes to steam, then peel and remove seeds.
  2. Roast the remaining vegetables
    • Roast tomatoes, onions and garlic at 200Â°C until softened and slightly caramelized (about 25â€“30 minutes).
  3. Chop and combine
    • Chop all vegetables to similar sizes and combine the charred peppers with the roasted vegetables in a large bowl.
  4. Dress and finish
    • Whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, caraway seeds, salt and pepper, then toss with the vegetables. Garnish with chopped parsley and mint and serve at room temperature.

Ingrediants

Eggs poached in spiced tomato sauce, a beloved Tunisian breakfast.

  1. Sauce
    • 800g crushed tomatoes
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tbsp harissa
    • 1 tsp caraway seeds
    • 1 tsp paprika
    • Olive oil
    • Salt and pepper
  2. Eggs & Toppings
    • 8 eggs
    • Fresh parsley
    • Crusty bread for serving
How to cook
  1. Build the spiced tomato sauce
    • Heat olive oil in a skillet and sautÃ© onion and garlic until soft.
    • Add harissa, paprika and caraway seeds and cook 30â€“60 seconds to release aromas.
    • Pour in crushed tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 12â€“15 minutes until slightly reduced.
  2. Poach the eggs
    • Create small wells in the sauce and gently crack the eggs into each well.
    • Cover the pan and cook 5â€“8 minutes until egg whites are set but yolks remain soft (adjust timing to your preference).
  3. Finish and serve
    • Remove from heat, sprinkle with chopped parsley and a drizzle of olive oil.
    • Serve straight from the pan with crusty bread for dipping.
